A. Ibarra

Character Name Juan Crisóstomo Ibarra y Magsalin Representation The privileged Filipino youth seeking justice against the Spanish
Appearance
  • Mestizo
  • Handsome
  • Young
 Main Description Ibarra is the son of one of the richest men in San Diego, Don Rafael. He has recently came back to the Philippines because of his father's death after studying in Europe for 7 years. During his time in the country, he observes the opression the people of his town face as well as the many injustices the Church brings upon them. In effort to overthrow and fix the system, he follows in his father's footsteps and builds San Diego's first secular school.
Traits
  • Educated
  • Sophisticated
  • Patriotic
  • Courageous
Relations
  • Enemy of Padre Damaso
  • Fiance of Maria Clara
  • Friend of Tenyente Guevarra

B. Maria Clara

Character Name Maria Clara Representation Rizal's idea of what a true Filipina should be like
Appearance
  • Filipina
  • Beautiful
  • Young
 Main Description Maria Clara is the main love interest of Ibarra. She was childhood friends with him before they went their seperate ways. While Ibarra studied in Europe, she studied in a convent. She came back to San Diego just as Ibarra returned. She was raised by Kapitan Tiago and his cousin, Tia Isabel. However, she finds out that her true father was Padre Damaso.
Traits
  • King-Hearted
  • Innocent
  • Loving
  • Well-loved
Relations
  • Daughter of Padre Damaso and Dona Pia
  • Step Daughter of Kapitan Tiago
  • Fiance of Ibarra

C. Father Damaso

Character Name Father Damaso Representation The epitome of the cruely of the Spanish Friars
Appearance
  • Spanish
  • Old
  • Gray-Haired
 Main Description Father Damaso is one of the main antagonists in the story. He is described as arrogant, selfish, and judgemental. Before he was transferred, he was the one who ordered Don Rafael's body to be exhumed from the Catholic Cemetary just because he refused to adhere to the absurd ways of the church. He is one of the most powerful figures in San Diego despite not being able to speak Tagalog. Furthermore, Padre Damaso is also the real father of Maria Clara.
Traits
  • Arrogant
  • Judgemental
  • Corrupt
 Relations
  • Father of Maria Clara
  • Enemy of Ibarra
  • Previous main friar of San Diego

D. Kapitan Tiago

Character Name Kapitan Tiago Representation Wealthy and powerful Filipinos bowing to the Spanish to secure their privilege
Appearance
  • Filipino
  • Chubby
  • Face has distortions due to Tobacco use
 Main Description Kapitan Tiago is one of the notable side characters of the story. He is a son of a merchant who obtained his riches by investing in land and tobacco monopoly. He makes his connections by being submissive towards those in power such as Padre Damaso. He is one of the biggest names in San Diego and is one of the most respected individuals due to his generous donations and parties.
Traits
  • Submissive
  • Spineless
  • Respected
Relations
  • Stepfather of Maria Clara
  • Husband of Dona Pia
  • Close friends of Padre Damaso and Salvi

E. Father Salvi

Character Name Father Salvi Representation The oppression and abuse of power of the church
Appearance
  • Spanish
  • Young
  • Thin
 Main Description Father Salvi is the friar of San Diego as well as one of the main villains in the novel. He was assigned there after Father Damaso's position was revoked. In the novel, he was known for his rivalry with the Alferez. Compared to Father Damaso, he doesn't resort to physical violence as a punishment of the sinners, instead, he asks them to pay huge amounts of money to "cleanse them of their sins" when in reality, he uses the funds for his own use.
Traits
  • Cunning
  • Perverse
  • Corrupt
 Relations
  • Rival of the town Alferez
  • In love with Maria Clara
  • Enemy of Ibarra

F. Lieutenant Guevarra

Character Name Miguel Guevarra Representation The government and its opposition towards the church
Appearance
  • Spanish
  • Old
Main Description Lieutenant Guevarra is one of the first characters introduced in the novel and is considered one of the allies of Ibarra. He respected Don Rafael to the highest regard and did eveything in his power to get him out of jail. Lieutenant resents the influence and power the Church holds and isn't blind to the way they abuse it. He tells Ibarra about what truly happened to Don Rafael and advised him to be privy of the actions of his and those around him
Traits
  • Respectful
  • Sophisticated
  • Caring
Relations
  • Enemy of Padre Damaso
  • Close friend of Don Rafael
  • Almost like a father figure to Ibarra

G. Sisa

Character Name Narcisa Representation The lowest of the low in the social class and the people who are negatively impacted by both the church and state as well as its rivalry.
Appearance
  • Filipina
  • Young
  • Frail
Main Description Sisa is a mother of 2 children, Crispin and Basilio, and the wife of Pedro. Sisa has great love for her children but despite that love, stays with her abusive husband who has no contribution to the family. She spends most of her time working to support her family which as a result, gives her no free time to visit the church which gains her a bad reputation.
Traits
  • Caring
  • Submissive
  • Loving
 Relations
  • Mother of Crispin and Basilio
  • Wife of Pedro

These are the first few characters introduced in the novel. The characters represent the dynamics of colonial society and the people in it.
Learning about the characters in the novel deepens one's understanding of society under the Spanish colonization and the reality of the situation countless Filipinos faced.
